What the Market Is Paying For Right Now
1. AI Data Center Physical Infrastructure (dominant theme — 7/10 winners)
The regime's center of gravity. VRT, DELL, AAOI, GLW, VIAV, FORM, FSLY all sell into the physical layer of AI compute: power/thermal, servers, optical transceivers, fiber, network test equipment, semiconductor probe cards, edge delivery. $630B+ in committed hyperscaler capex is the macro driver. The market treats disclosed backlogs and multiyear contracts as hard proof — VRT's $15B backlog, DELL's $43B backlog, GLW's $6B Meta deal. Names with financial proof sustain momentum; narrative-only AI names face durability risk.
2. Forward Guidance That Shatters Street Models (the mechanism that drives re-rating)
The beat itself is secondary. What forces sustained repricing is the guide gap — the spread between management's forward guidance and pre-print consensus. Effective guide gaps this cycle: DELL +$14.5B revenue / +$1.31 EPS above Street. VRT +13% EPS above Street. FORM +10.3% revenue / +45% EPS above Street. FSLY +$42M revenue / +86% EPS above Street. VIAV +10% revenue / +35% EPS above Street. DVA +11% EPS above Street. FIGS +12% revenue gap. Median effective guide gap: ~10–13% above consensus.
3. Revenue Growth That Is Accelerating, Not Just Positive
Every AI infrastructure winner showed the growth rate itself getting faster: VRT (26% → 27–29% guided), DELL (19% FY → 39% Q4 → 51% Q1 guided), FORM (2.8% FY → 13.6% Q4 → 31.3% Q1 guided), AAOI (QoQ: ~1% → 13% → guided 17%), FSLY (15% FY → 23% Q4), VIAV (2.3% 5yr CAGR → 36.4%). Steady-state growth gets a nod. Accelerating growth gets a re-rating.
4. Margin Expansion Concurrent With Revenue Growth
The strongest inflection pattern: expanding top line AND expanding margins simultaneously. FORM (+540bps GM in two quarters), AAOI (+580bps GM YoY), FSLY (+650bps GM YoY to 64%), VIAV (+440bps op margin YoY), VRT (+170bps op margin YoY), GLW (+180bps op margin YoY). Margin compression on revenue growth (INGM, DELL ISG segment) is a yellow flag even when the top line is explosive.
5. Profitability Inflections at Cheap, Thinly Covered Names
Loss-to-profit turnarounds at low multiples with ≤6 analysts were aggressively rewarded. ECPG: $139M loss → $257M profit, 51% EPS beat, 5.1x forward P/E, 3 analysts, 9% buyback — the regime prototype. FORM: 38.5% → 43.9% GM, 4–5 analysts. FSLY: first profitable year, limited coverage. Direction of profitability change matters more than absolute level. The thin coverage creates a longer revision cycle because fewer analysts = slower model updates = more persistent mispricing.
